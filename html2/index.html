Gyeongbokgung Palace (1395)

This palace is one of five located in Seoul. It was the main palace during the Josean dynasty. It is the first and largest palace built during this dynasty, representing the sovereignty it. The palace Hours vary by season, but it is typically open from 9 a.m. to 17:00/ 18:30. Admission is 3,000 won (~$2.69) for adults and 1,500 won (~ $1.35) for visitors age 7 to 18. You can also rent hanboks or other outfits to wear for photoshoots. (From Wikipedia)

Myeongdong Shopping Street

This shopping street is one of, if not the most famous street/neighborhood in Seoul. Stores generally open from 11:00 to 22:00, and there are also night market stalls where you can get food, sweets, skincare products, etc. (From Local Insider)

Han River

The Han River is located in Northern South Korea and goes through Seoul. Some activites you can do here are boat tours, yacht rides, jet skiing, etc. Many locals also have picnics because of the nice scenary and vibe. Bike rides are another common activity in this area. It is easy to get there through public transportation as well making it a desireable place to go to. ( Other Activities)

HYBE Building (2021)

This building is an entertainment company's build of many popular kpop groups such as BTS and TXT. It is 19 stories, alongside 7 underground floors. The building contains a museum on levels B1 and B2 that contain many of its artists' achievements. You can buy tickets on the online website through Weverse. Tickets are 22,000won(~$20). They also have a museum shop where you can buy souvenirs or other products. (Link to their website)
